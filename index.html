Smarter drone monitoring for timber plantations

DIFF is a prototype system that uses drones and simple AI to monitor tree health, detect problems early, and reduce manual field work for plantation teams.

  • Fly over assets instead of walking every block in the field.
  • Capture consistent images of tree rows, blocks and boundaries.
  • Turn raw images into simple maps and alerts that non-technical users can read.

About DIFF

Overview of how DIFF supports plantation monitoring and management.

Many plantation teams rely on manual patrols and paper notes to understand what is happening in the field. This is slow, tiring, and makes it easy to miss early signs of disease, water stress or illegal logging. DIFF explores whether a simple drone and web app can give a clearer picture with less effort.

Objective
O

What we want to prove

Build a working prototype that shows how images from a drone can be:

  • captured in a structured way,
  • organised by plantation block, and
  • viewed on a simple, non-technical dashboard.
Pain points
P

Problems we focus on

DIFF concentrates on three practical issues faced by plantation teams:

  • long inspection time in the field,
  • lack of up-to-date visual records,
  • difficulty linking field issues to decisions.
Prototype scope
S

What we will show

  • a physical or simulated drone with a simple sensor mount,
  • a small sample of plantation images or test data,
  • a web page that displays blocks, alerts and notes.
D

Drone concept – what DIFF flies with

The DIFF drone concept focuses on stable, repeatable flights over tree rows. It does not need to be a very expensive platform. The drone follows a route, captures overlapping images and returns safely with usable data.

  • Standard quadcopter platform with GPS-assisted flight modes.
  • Camera mounted to see tree crowns and ground clearly.
  • Optional extra sensor (temperature / humidity / simple multispectral).
  • Flight plan based on plantation blocks, not random manual flying.
A

Web & app – how people see the data

The DIFF web and mobile views act as a control centre for plantation staff. Users can quickly see which blocks are healthy, which need checking, and basic statistics from recent flights.

  • Overview of total blocks, flights and alerts.
  • Map or grid view of plantation zones.
  • Detail page for each block with photos and notes.
  • Reserved space for future features such as trends or AI scores.
